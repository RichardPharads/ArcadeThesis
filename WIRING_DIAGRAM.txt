PIN LAYOUT REFERENCE:
====================

Raspberry Pi 40-pin GPIO Header:

    3V3  (1) (2)  5V  ←── VCC
   GPIO2  (3) (4)  5V
   GPIO3  (5) (6)  GND ←── GND
   GPIO4  (7) (8)  GPIO14
     GND  (9) (10) GPIO15
  GPIO17 (11) (12) GPIO18 ←── TRIG
  GPIO27 (13) (14) GND
  GPIO22 (15) (16) GPIO23
    3V3 (17) (18) GPIO24 ←── ECHO
  GPIO10 (19) (20) GND
   GPIO9 (21) (22) GPIO25
  GPIO11 (23) (24) GPIO8
     GND (25) (26) GPIO7
   GPIO0 (27) (28) GPIO1
   GPIO5 (29) (30) GND
   GPIO6 (31) (32) GPIO12
  GPIO13 (33) (34) GND
  GPIO19 (35) (36) GPIO16
  GPIO26 (37) (38) GPIO20
     GND (39) (40) GPIO21

WIRING CHECKLIST:
================
□ VCC (HC-SR04) → Pin 2 (5V) on Pi
□ GND (HC-SR04) → Pin 6 (GND) on Pi  
□ Trig (HC-SR04) → Pin 12 (GPIO 18) on Pi
□ Echo (HC-SR04) → Voltage Divider → Pin 18 (GPIO 24) on Pi

VOLTAGE DIVIDER CIRCUIT (REQUIRED):
===================================
Echo Pin → 1kΩ Resistor → GPIO 24 (Pi)
                    ↓
                2kΩ Resistor
                    ↓
                   GND

⚠️  WARNING: Resistors are REQUIRED to protect Pi GPIO pins!
    HC-SR04 outputs 5V, but Pi GPIO pins are 3.3V only.

TESTING STEPS:
=============
1. Power on Raspberry Pi
2. Connect sensor as shown above
3. Run: python3 ultrasonic_detector.py
4. Place hand/object within 30cm of sensor
5. Verify "OBSTACLE DETECTED!" message
6. Check that main.py launches automatically
